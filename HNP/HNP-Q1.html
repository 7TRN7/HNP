JAVASCRIPT OPERATORS

There are 4 javascript operators Comparison Operators, Arithmetic Operators, Logical Operators, and Assignment Operators.

A. Arithmetic Operators are used to perform mathematical expressions on numbers.

(+) produces the sum of numeric operands or string concatenation.
(-) subtracts the two operands, producing their difference.
(*) produces the product of the operands.
(**) returns the result of raising the first operand to the power of the second operand.
(/) produces the quotient of its operands where the left operand is the dividend and the right operand is the divisor.
(%) returns the remainder left over when one operand is divided by a second operand.
(++) increments (adds one to) its operand and returns a value.increments (adds one to) its operand and returns a value.
(--) decrements (subtracts one from) its operand and returns a value.

B. Logical Operators are used to combine two or more conditions between variables or values.

(&&) is used when there are two variables.
(||) is used when you have something to pick from.
(!) Is used when you want to exclude a variable.

C. Assignment Operators are used to assign values to variables.

(=) Assigns a value to a variable
(+=) Adds a value to a variable
(-=) Subtracts a value from a variable
(*=) Multiplies a variable
(/=) Divides a variable
(%=) Assigns a remainder to a variable

D. Comparison Operators are used to compare numbers or strings and perform evaluations.

(==) Equal
(===) Equal value and equal type
(!=) not equal
(!==) not equal value or not equal type
(>) greater than
(<) less than
(>=) greater than or equal to
(<=) less than or equal to
